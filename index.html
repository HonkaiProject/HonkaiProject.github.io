Welcome to here

YoonKi Kim

I am a researcher in the Department of Mathematics and Department of Physics. I'm a researcher working on the ring singularity of Kerr black holes, quantum physics, number theory, and calculus.

My research interests center on the theoretical exploration of black hole dynamics and their evolutionary pathways, with a focus on energy extraction mechanisms and mathematical conjectures in extreme gravitational environments. To investigate these, I employ analytical methods, thought experiments, and novel mathematical frameworks, such as those presented in my works on Kerr-to-Schwarzschild transitions, the Riemann Hypothesis, and the Yang-Mills mass gap. My approach involves deriving fundamental relationships (e.g., angular momentum conservation, zeta function zeros, and gauge-invariant path integrals), validating them through rigorous proofs or numerical simulations, and interpreting their implications for astrophysics, number theory, and quantum field theory. Recently I'm interests in Partial Differential Equations and Fluid Dynamics.

Research Interests

  • Quantum Field Theory and Yang-Mills Theory
  • Analytic Number Theory and the Riemann Hypothesis
  • Partial Differential Equations and Fluid Dynamics
