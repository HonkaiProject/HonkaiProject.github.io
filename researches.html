Ongoing Projects

Black Hole Evolution: From Kerr to Schwarzschild

To understand the evolution of rotating black holes, it is important to study the dynamics of angular momentum and energy extraction. Using theoretical thought experiments, I am investigating how the Penrose process affects the rotational energy of Kerr black holes and exploring the possibility of their transition into Schwarzschild black holes.

Quantum Field Theory: Yang-Mills Mass Gap

The Yang-Mills mass gap problem is a fundamental challenge in quantum field theory. Using a novel Alpha Integration framework, I am working on proving the existence of a mass gap in SU(N) Yang-Mills theory, which has implications for quark-gluon confinement and the broader understanding of quantum field dynamics.

Analytic Number Theory: Riemann Hypothesis

The Riemann Hypothesis is one of the most famous unsolved problems in mathematics. I am developing an inductive proof method to demonstrate that all non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function lie on the critical line Re(s) = 0.5, using a combination of analytical and numerical techniques.

Resolving the Navier-Stokes Existence and Smoothness Problem Using Alpha Integration

This paper, authored by YoonKi Kim and dated April 11, 2025, tackles one of the Clay Mathematics Institute's Millennium Prize Problems: the existence, uniqueness, and smoothness of solutions to the Navier-Stokes equations for incompressible fluids in two and three dimensions. Leveraging the innovative Alpha Integration framework, the author rigorously addresses the challenging nonlinear term (u·∇)u, ensuring convergence through a universal exponential suppression measure. The paper proves that solutions u and p are infinitely smooth (C∞), satisfy bounded energy conditions, and exist uniquely for initial data in H¹ and L² spaces, even with non-zero external forces. By optimizing path selection and providing explicit convergence constants, the study excludes finite-time blow-up and extends to distributional initial conditions, offering a comprehensive resolution to the problem. With detailed analyses of pressure regularity and practical insights into fluid dynamics applications, this work marks a significant advancement in mathematical physics, poised to influence turbulence modeling and computational fluid dynamics.
