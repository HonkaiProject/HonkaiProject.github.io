Ongoing Projects

Black Hole Evolution: From Kerr to Schwarzschild

To understand the evolution of rotating black holes, it is important to study the dynamics of angular momentum and energy extraction. Using theoretical thought experiments, I am investigating how the Penrose process affects the rotational energy of Kerr black holes and exploring the possibility of their transition into Schwarzschild black holes.

Quantum Field Theory: Yang-Mills Mass Gap

The Yang-Mills mass gap problem is a fundamental challenge in quantum field theory. Using a novel Alpha Integration framework, I am working on proving the existence of a mass gap in SU(N) Yang-Mills theory, which has implications for quark-gluon confinement and the broader understanding of quantum field dynamics.

Analytic Number Theory: Riemann Hypothesis

The Riemann Hypothesis is one of the most famous unsolved problems in mathematics. I am developing an inductive proof method to demonstrate that all non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function lie on the critical line Re(s) = 0.5, using a combination of analytical and numerical techniques.
