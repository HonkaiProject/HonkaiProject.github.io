  • From Kerr to Schwarzschild: A Thought Experiment on Black Hole Evolution
YoonKi Kim | April 9, 2025

This paper presents a thought experiment on the conservation of angular momentum in Kerr black holes, focusing on the Penrose process as a mechanism for energy extraction. We derive the relationships between the black hole's mass, angular momentum, and extractable energy, highlighting the impact of negative-energy particle ejection on rotational dynamics. For a solar-mass Kerr black hole, we calculate the ring singularity radius and demonstrate that up to 29.29% of the mass-energy can be extracted while preserving angular momentum constraints. The study suggests a novel evolutionary pathway: a Kerr black hole may transition into a Schwarzschild black hole upon complete rotational energy depletion.

  • Resolving the Yang-Mills Mass Gap Problem Using Alpha Integration
YoonKi Kim | March 10, 2025

We introduce Alpha Integration, a novel path integral framework that applies universally to a wide range of functions and spaces while preserving gauge invariance. This method resolves the Yang-Mills mass gap problem by proving a positive lowest eigenvalue (E₀ > 0) for the SU(N) Yang-Mills Hamiltonian in four-dimensional Euclidean spacetime, demonstrating a mass gap and quark-gluon confinement. Alpha Integration offers a robust alternative to traditional path integral techniques, with applications across theoretical and applied sciences.

  • An Inductive Proof of the Riemann Hypothesis
YoonKi Kim | April 4, 2025

This paper presents a rigorous inductive proof that all non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function lie on the critical line Re(s) = 0.5, proving the Riemann Hypothesis. Using a novel "impossibility of zero migration" method and exhaustive analytical and numerical validations, we demonstrate that zeros cannot deviate from σ = 0.5 within the critical strip (0 < σ < 1), establishing the hypothesis's truth.

  • Resolving the Navier-Stokes Existence and Smoothness Problem Using Alpha Integration
YoonKi Kim | April 12, 2025

This 2025 paper addresses the Clay Millennium Problem on the Navier-Stokes equations using the Alpha Integration framework. It proves the existence, uniqueness, and infinite smoothness of solutions in 2D and 3D for H¹ and L² initial data, handling non-zero forces and ruling out blow-up. By optimizing paths and quantifying convergence, it advances fluid dynamics and turbulence modeling.
